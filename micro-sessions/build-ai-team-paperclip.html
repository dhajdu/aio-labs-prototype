03 Concept

The Bottleneck Is You

The constraint in most teams is not capability. It is capacity. There is only one of you. You can work faster, or you can work smarter. But there is a third option.

Dana Reyes at Buddy Bevs is launching BOLT nationally. She needs competitor research, launch copy, retail partner outreach, and a performance briefing for leadership - all this week. She has three people. She needs ten.

One person, sequential

Dana researches competitors Monday. Writes copy Tuesday. Analyzes data Wednesday. Misses the Thursday leadership meeting with an incomplete picture.

One person + AI team, parallel

Dana sets the goal Sunday night. By Monday morning, research is done, copy is drafted, and data is summarized. She spends Monday making decisions.

Key Insight

The bottleneck is not how fast you work. It is how many things you can run at once. Paperclip removes that ceiling.

04 How It Works

What Paperclip Does

Paperclip is an open-source orchestration tool. It is the operating system for your AI team. You create a company, hire agents, assign each one a role and a budget, set a top-level goal, and Paperclip coordinates the rest.

It already has 31,000+ GitHub stars. Founders are using it to run entire business functions with small teams. The tagline says it clearly: your agents don't need better prompts. They need an org chart.

The four things you set up

1

Your company

The container that holds everything. One company per project or goal. Complete data isolation between companies.

2

Your agents

Each agent gets a role. Research. Copy. Analytics. Whatever the work requires. You bring the agents - Paperclip manages them.

3

Your goal

One clear goal at the top. Paperclip breaks it into tasks, assigns them to the right agents, and tracks progress from a single dashboard.

4

Your budget

Every agent gets a monthly budget. When they hit it, they stop automatically. No runaway costs. No surprises.

Setup

Install Paperclip with one command: npx paperclipai onboard --yes — then follow the prompts. Your first company is live in about five minutes.

05 Example

Dana's BOLT Team

Dana's goal: launch BOLT into three new markets in 60 days. Here is the Paperclip team she builds.

R

Research Agent Research

Scan competitor pricing, distribution channels, and market positioning in Jakarta, Lagos, and Bogota. Produce a one-page summary per market with the top three opportunities and top three risks.

C

Copy Agent Creation

Using the research summary, write a localized launch email for retail partners in each market. Reference the local context. Do not send the same email to all three markets.

A

Analytics Agent Analytics

Pull the BOLT pilot data. Identify the top-performing customer segments by repurchase rate and average spend. Write a one-paragraph executive summary for the Thursday leadership meeting.

Key Insight

Dana is not the researcher, writer, or analyst. She is the manager. She set the goal, designed the team, and will review the outputs. That is the AI Officer role.

06 Exercise
Everyone Does This Now

Map Your AI Team

Take two minutes. Think about a real goal you are working toward - something that needs to move in the next two weeks.

Map three agents. Give each one a role and one sentence describing their specific task. Then post it in the chat.

Not sure where to start? Try Dana's pattern: one agent researches, one creates, one analyzes. Most goals need all three.

What makes a strong team map

Weak

Agent 1: help with marketing. Agent 2: do research. Agent 3: write things. These briefs are too vague to run.

Strong

Agent 1: scan competitor pricing in two markets and summarize risks. Agent 2: write three localized launch emails. Agent 3: analyze last quarter's data and surface the top segment.

07 Leadership

The Manager's Mindset

When you build a Paperclip team, your job changes. You are not the researcher, the writer, or the analyst. You are the manager. You set the goal. You define what good looks like. You review the output and decide what ships.

That is a leadership role. And it is exactly what organizations will need as AI becomes half of every workflow.

Key Insight

An AI Officer does not use more tools. They manage better teams. Paperclip is where that shift becomes real.

09 Takeaways

Key Takeaways

  • The bottleneck is capacity, not capability You are not slow. There is just one of you. A Paperclip team removes the ceiling on how many things you can run at once.
  • Your agents need an org chart, not better prompts Coordination is the unlock. When each agent has a clear role, a specific task, and a shared goal, the output is faster and better than any single agent working alone.
  • The manager's job is goal-setting and review You set the goal. You design the team. You review the outputs and decide what ships. That is the AI Officer role - and it is a leadership role.
  • The map comes before the build The team map exercise is the real work. If you can describe each agent's role in one sentence, you are ready to build. If you cannot, keep thinking.
10 Try This Week

Try This Week

Install Paperclip. Build your team. Run it on a real goal. The first build always takes longer than you expect - but the second one takes half the time, because you already know how to think in teams.

Once you have an output you are happy with, share it with your manager or a colleague. Show them the team map and what it produced. Ask what else it could run on. That is how a personal AI team becomes an organizational capability.

Pro Tip

Start with a goal you already know well. Your first Paperclip build should not be a learning experience about the goal - only about the tool. Use something familiar so you can judge whether the output is good.
